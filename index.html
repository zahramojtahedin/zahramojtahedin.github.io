Research

Selected research projects in neural networks, neural engineering, biomedical sensing, and intelligent hardware.

Optimization of ReLU-Based Layer Architectures in Deep Neural Networks Using Approximation Theory

Master’s Thesis · Supervisor: Prof. Siavash Bayat

This thesis develops a theory-driven framework for designing neural network architectures using approximation theory. It studies how depth, width, connectivity, and network complexity influence approximation capability and accuracy.

Deep Neural Networks ReLU Approximation Theory

TransMorph-Based Modeling of Zebrafish Brain Activity and Behavior

Research Project · Supervisor: Prof. Quan Wen

Research on neural recording, brain activity modeling, behavioral analysis, and embedded hardware for animal studies. The work includes neural data acquisition, sensor development, and computational modeling.

Neural Recording Embedded Hardware Brain Imaging

Detection of Human Concentration Using Eyelid Movements and Neural Signals

Bachelor’s Project · Supervisor: Prof. Saeed Bagheri Shouraki

A non-invasive concentration monitoring system based on eye tracking, eyelid and pupil behavior, and EEG signals. An ESP32-based eye-tracking setup and custom image-processing algorithms were used to estimate cognitive attention levels.

EEG Eye Tracking ESP32 Machine Learning

Smart Wearable Health-Monitoring System

Embedded and Biomedical Systems Project

Development of a multi-sensor wearable device capable of monitoring SpO₂, heart rate, ECG, and patient status. The system is based on an STM32 microcontroller and uses LoRaWAN for long-range transmission.

STM32 ECG SpO₂ LoRaWAN
